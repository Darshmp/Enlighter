Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common queries about the course

Do I need prior programming experience for this course?

While prior programming experience is helpful, it's not strictly required. We include a Python crash course in the first week that will get you up to speed with the programming fundamentals needed for the rest of the course. Many of our successful students started with no programming background.

What's the time commitment required for this course?

The course is designed to be completed in 8 weeks with about 8-10 hours of study per week. However, you'll have lifetime access to all materials, so you can learn at your own pace if needed. The flexible structure makes it suitable for working professionals.

What kind of support will I receive during the course?

You'll have access to multiple support channels: weekly live Q&A sessions with instructors, a dedicated student community forum where you can ask questions, and email support for technical issues. Our team is committed to helping you succeed throughout your learning journey.

Will this course help me get a job in AI/ML?

Absolutely! The course includes career preparation modules covering resume building, portfolio development, and interview preparation. Many of our students have successfully transitioned into AI/ML roles after completing the course. We also have partnerships with tech companies that often recruit from our alumni network.

What software/tools will I need for this course?

You'll need a computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux) with at least 8GB RAM. We'll guide you through installing Python, Jupyter Notebooks, and other essential libraries (all free and open-source). For the more advanced projects, we recommend using Google Colab which provides free GPU access.
